Who We Are

Company Overview

Five things every new joiner should know about Impact Analytics on their first day.

01

Our Mission & Story

"Clarity From Chaos With Agentic AI"

Founded by retail and technology veterans, Impact Analytics builds AI-native software that helps the world's leading brands plan smarter, price better, and run leaner.

  • Founded in 2015, headquartered in Atlanta, GA · CEO: Prashant Agrawal
  • 150+ clients across 20+ countries
  • $2B+ in value delivered to clients
  • 60% reduction in lost sales for our clients
  • 2M+ machine learning models running in production
02

What We Do

AI-Native · Retail-Expert · Results-Driven

We build Agentic AI software that goes beyond surfacing insights — our autonomous agents act on those insights, monitor operations continuously, and drive proactive decisions.

  • Covers merchandise planning, demand forecasting, inventory optimisation, and pricing & promotions
  • Humans stay in control — AI does the heavy lifting, people retain final decision authority
  • Result: driving the enterprise proactively, not looking backward at historical data
03

Our Culture & Values

We're a globally distributed team that runs on curiosity, ownership, and a genuine love of solving hard problems.

  • Innovate Boldly — AI-native thinking at every layer
  • Obsess Over Client Impact — $2B+ in value delivered; client outcomes are our north star
  • Grow Fast — you are joining at an exciting moment
  • Own It — take initiative, don't wait to be told
04

Industries & Clients

Impact Analytics serves clients across six major verticals.

  • Retail — Apparel, Footwear, Specialty, Department, Furniture, Electronics
  • Grocery — Minimise waste, optimise delivery, maximise on-shelf availability
  • Manufacturing — AI-led demand estimation
  • Wholesale & CPG — Confidence amid shifting buyer behaviours
  • QSR — Real-time clarity across demand, pricing, and service
  • Trusted by: Victoria's Secret · Under Armour · Levi's · Coach · Ralph Lauren · PacSun · Dollar General · Patagonia & 100+ more

18 Days Annual Leave12 Days Sick Leave5 Public + 5 Festival Holidays Apply via KEKA
  • Annual Leave: 18 days per calendar year, with 4.5 days allocated per quarter.
  • Carryover: Up to 45 days of unused annual leave can be carried forward.
  • Casual & Sick Leave: 12 days per year, 3 days per quarter. Cannot be carried forward.
  • Public Holidays: 5 mandatory national holidays + 5 festival holidays announced annually.
  • Maternity Leave: 26 weeks (8 before + 18 after delivery). 12 weeks for 2+ children.
  • Paternity Leave: 5 days, within 3 months of birth or adoption.
  • Notice period: No leave permitted. Pre-approved leaves are automatically cancelled.
  • How to apply: Raise all requests through the KEKA portal.
12% Employee + 12% EmployerAction Required on Day 1
  • What is EPF? Both you and the company each contribute 12% of basic salary every month.
  • UAN: Your permanent PF identity number. Share existing UAN with HR immediately on joining.
  • Activate your UAN at the EPFO member portal using Aadhaar, PAN, or Member ID.
  • Link Aadhaar to UAN: Go to Manage → KYC in the EPFO portal — mandatory for all transactions.
  • Documents to submit: Aadhaar card, PAN card, and bank account details with IFSC code.
  • Nominate a family member on the EPFO portal (Manage → e-Nomination).

Your First Week Playbook

New Joiner Survival Guide

Everything you need to land confidently, build relationships fast, and set yourself up for success from Day 1.

Your Workspace Setup

Your desk, laptop, and access card are all set up and waiting for you. Head to your designated seat and log in with the credentials shared by IT.

Office Tour

You'll get a quick walkthrough of the office — meeting rooms, cafeteria, IT helpdesk, and all the spots you'll need to know.

Your Laptop & Accounts

Your laptop, email account, and Slack workspace will be set up and waiting. IT will walk you through the setup during the first hour.

Meet Your Buddy

You've been assigned an onboarding buddy — a friendly face from your team. They'll have lunch with you and answer all your questions.

Onboarding Session

A structured 2-hour session covers policies, tools, team introductions, and company culture. No hard stuff on Day 1.

Lunch Is On Us

Day 1 lunch is hosted by the HR team. Use this time to get to know the people you'll be building with.

1
Monday — Day 1

Welcome, Orientation & Setup

Collect your laptop, complete HR documentation, attend the onboarding orientation, meet your buddy, and have lunch with the team.

2
Tuesday — Day 2

Tools, Access & Product Intro

Get all system access sorted (Jira, Confluence, GitHub, Slack). Attend the Product Introduction session to understand what module you'll be working on.

3
Wednesday — Day 3

Deep-dive: Codebase / Domain

Your buddy walks you through the repo structure, coding standards, and deployment pipeline. Start reading existing PRs and architecture documents.

4
Thursday — Day 4

Shadow & Ask Questions

Sit in on sprint ceremonies. Shadow your buddy's work, ask as many questions as you can, and map out the people you need to know.

5
Friday — Day 5

Your First Task & Week 1 Retro

Pick up your first small ticket and make your first contribution. Meet with your team lead to reflect on Week 1 and align on goals for Week 2.

Ask Early, Ask Often

No question is too basic in the first month. The fastest learners ask the most questions. Use Slack threads, buddy sessions, and your 1:1s proactively.

Keep a Learning Journal

Jot down what you learn each day — tools, processes, team dynamics. You'll look back on it with pride.

Own Your Calendar

Block focus time early in the week. Show up on time to every meeting. Respond to meeting invites promptly.

Default To Async

Use Slack threads and Confluence over long meetings. Be clear and concise in written communication.

Build Relationships

Take 15 minutes to have a virtual coffee with someone new every week. Cross-team relationships will be your biggest advantage.

Close the Loop Always

When you finish a task or resolve a query, say so. "Done" is one of the most appreciated words at Impact Analytics.
